## 1) Robustness of Agentic AI Systems via Adversarially-Aligned Jacobian Regularization
- **Authors:** (see arXiv)  
- **arXiv:** `2603.04378v1` (**cs.LG**, cs.AI, cs.CR, cs.MA)

**Summary (5-8 lines):**
- Proposes a robustness training/regularization method for agentic systems using Jacobian alignment under adversarial perturbations.  
- Targets failure modes where small prompt/context shifts cause large action-policy deviations in tool-using agents.  
- Frames agent reliability as local smoothness + adversarial consistency in representation/action mappings.  
- Evaluates robustness deltas under attack-like perturbations rather than only clean-task success.  
- Reports improved stability against adversarially crafted contexts compared with baseline tuning methods.  
- Emphasizes robustness as a control primitive for safety-critical agent deployment.

**Novelty:**
- Extends Jacobian-based robustness ideas into **agentic action pipelines** rather than static prediction tasks.
- Couples adversarial alignment objectives with agent behavior stability metrics.

**Security relevance:**
- **Permissions/policy dimension:** More stable policy execution under adversarial prompts helps prevent policy-violating tool actions.
- **Why it matters:** Robustness at the policy boundary can reduce exploitability of injection-style perturbations.

**Limitations / open questions:**
- Transferability to real multi-tool orchestration stacks is unclear.
- May trade off capability/efficiency for smoothness constraints.
- Needs evaluation on indirect prompt injection benchmarks.

**Tags:** `agent robustness`, `adversarial defense`, `policy stability`, `tool-use safety`, `jacobian regularization`
